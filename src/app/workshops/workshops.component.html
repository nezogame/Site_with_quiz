Our Workshops

At our meetings during informal learning, young people learned a lot of interesting information about politics, democracy, and elections to the European Parliament. With each meeting, we saw how young people’s interest in politics grew.
At our meetings there were speakers who were specialists in the field of Politics and International Relations, such as members of the European Parliament, US Congress Woman, deputies of the Youth Seimas of Latvia, advisors of the United Nations, students of Columbia University and the University of Maine.
We saw that with each meeting we were moving towards our goal, we saw how young people participate in discussions and ask interesting questions.

First Workshop

A small recap from our first meeting The participants got knowledge on political culture, western political Ideology and forms of government, contemporary democratic liberalism, levels of politics, political mobilisation and engagement

Had an informal discussion about voting age, direct&indirect democracy and biggest political issues.

Got tested on their knowledge and the 1st place winner got a book from our sponsor Janis Roze „Politika lesācejiem".

Thanks to our 1st speaker Eric Payerle. Also thanks to our 2nd speaker Natasha Wood for providing an inspirational.

Second Workshop

Grateful for the enriching experience at the Ireland Embassy event on 29.01.2024! More than 20 teenagers from different schools and gymnasiums came to listen to our speakers and take part in the discussion of important topics of Youth Political Society .

    Participants gained comprehensive insights into :

  • election structures,
  • effective climate policy,
  • public speech,
  • politics teaching in school system,
  • informal political talks for teenagers
  • discussions on voting age.

Special thanks to the three distinguished speakers Eric Payerle, Anastasija Baranova and Keegan Farrington for their thought-provoking perspectives.

Third Workshop

Grateful for the enriching experience at the SEB Inovaciju centrs on 29.02.2024! We discussed many important and interesting topics in our third Workshop:

  • How to improve understanding of society and youth political processes?
  • Should young people be allowed to vote in elections from the age of 16?
  • How to ensure a policy of generational change?
  • politics teaching in school system,
  • informal political talks for teenagers
  • discussions on voting age.
